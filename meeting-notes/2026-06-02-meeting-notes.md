## Discussion Notes

Key points raised during the meeting. Keep each note short and clear.

- **Four deliverables are due Monday, June 8.** These all belong to the project's inception phase, the first stage where we define what we are building and how we will work together:
  1. **Team Charter.** A short agreement that spells out our team norms: who does what, how we communicate, how we make decisions, and how we handle conflict. It is the ground rules for the whole semester.
  2. **Inception Presentation.** A slide deck that pitches the project. It covers the problem we are solving, who the users are, the scope, and the value the system delivers. This is how we present our vision to the class.
  3. **Artifacts: use cases, user stories, and domain model.** These are the core requirements documents. Use cases describe step by step how a user interacts with the system. User stories capture each requirement from the user's point of view in plain language. The domain model is a diagram of the key things in our problem area (the main entities) and how they relate to each other.
  4. **Cross-Team Requirements Review Packet.** A bundle of our requirements prepared so another team can review them. This supports the peer review process, where teams check each other's work and give feedback before the requirements are locked in.
- **Jeff will seed the documents from Professor Peruma's samples.** Jeff will copy the professor's sample documents into the shared Drive folder, reuse the document structure the professor proposed, and take a first pass at filling each one in. Starting from the provided structure keeps us aligned with what the course expects and saves us from designing the format ourselves. This is a first draft only, and everyone is free to change anything they do not like.
- **The Team Charter will be edited asynchronously before the next meeting.** Rather than write it together in real time, each person will add their own input on their own schedule. Everyone agreed to add one to two bullets to each section, and to finish this over the weekend (by Sunday, June 7) so the charter is ready ahead of the Monday deadline. With all members contributing, that should give us about five bullets per section, which is a solid amount of input without any one person carrying the whole thing. Jeff took a first stab at the long-form answers to give the team a starting point. If anyone dislikes what he wrote, they should feel free to delete it and rewrite it.
