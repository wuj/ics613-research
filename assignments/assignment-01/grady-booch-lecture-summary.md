The aphorism "not all architecture is design, but all design is architecture" distinguishes the architecturally weighty decisions (expensive to reverse) from ordinary ones. He stresses that good architecture isn't what makes a system cheap to build, but what makes it cheap to *evolve*. | "All architecture is design but not all design is architecture... significant is measured by cost of change" — [Wikiquote](https://en.wikiquote.org/wiki/Grady_Booch) |
